Get ready for Brext: Your results

What you need to do may change

Subscribe to updates about changes that may affect you and get a link to your results

What you need to do

Living in the UK

Because you said:

  • You are an EU national
  • You live in the UK

Apply to the EU Settlement Scheme by 31 December 2020 to continue living in the UK

If you do not apply to the Settlement Scheme, you may not be able to continue living or working in the UK as you do now.

Read the guidance: Apply to the EU Settlement Scheme (settled and pre-settled status)

Order your prescriptions as normal

You do not need to change how you order or take your medicines.

Read NHS guidance: Getting your medicines if there’s a no-deal Brexit

EU citizens do not need to prove settled status to work or rent

Right to work and right to rent checks remain unchanged until at least 30 December 2020.

Read NHS guidance:
  • Right to rent checks for EU nationals after Brexit
  • Employing EU nationals after Brexit

Visiting the UK

Because you said:

  • You are an EU national
  • You plan to travel to the UK

Take out appropriate travel insurance with health cover before travelling to the UK

You will be charged for your care if you do not have the right insurance.

Read the guidance: Healthcare after Brexit: citizens of the EU, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein and Switzerland visiting the UK

Moving back to the UK

Because you said:

  • You are a British national
  • You plan to move back to the UK

To get free NHS healthcare, prove that you’ve returned to the UK to live and you meet the ordinary residence test

If you can’t prove you meet the ordinary residence test, you may be charged for NHS services.

Read the guidance: Living in the EU: prepare for Brexit

Check you’re eligible for housing services if you’re moving back to the UK from the EU

You must check with your local council that you're eligible for housing services if you’re returning to the UK from the EU.

Read the guidance: Council housing

Studying in the UK

Because you said:

  • You are an EU national
  • You are studying in the UK

Contact your home university to check if you can continue studying on your Erasmus+ placement in the UK

If you have not already started your placement you may not be able to study in the UK. If you have started, you may not get NHS healthcare, student finance or benefits.

Living in the EU

Because you said:

  • You are a British national
  • You live in the EU

Apply to be a resident in the EU country you live in

You may not be able to continue living and using services in the EU if you are not a resident.

UK State Pension will continue to be paid if you live in an EEA state or Switzerland

You do not need to take any action to keep recieving your UK state pension after Brexit.

Read the guidance: Benefits and pensions for UK nationals in the EU

Visiting the EU

Because you said:

  • You are a British national
  • You plan to visit the EU

Check a passport for travel to Europe after Brexit

You may not be able to travel if you do not renew your passport in time.

Read the guidance: Passport rules for travel to Europe after Brexit

Contact your vet at least 4 months before travelling to make sure your pet is able to travel to the EU

{{ govukInsetText({ text: "It takes at least 4 months" }) }}

If your pet does not have the proper documents, you will not be able to take it with you.

Read the guidance: Pet travel to Europe after Brexit

Take out appropriate travel insurance with health cover before travelling to the EU

You may be charged for your care if you do not get health cover with your travel insurance.

Read the guidance: Foreign travel insurance

Studying in the EU

Because you said:

  • You are a British national
  • You study in the EU

Contact your university to check if you can continue studying in the EU

Changes to your residency, healthcare or tuition fee status may stop you from completing your course.

Read the guidance: UK students in the EU: continuing your studies

Selling goods and services

Because you said:

  • You own or operate a business or organisation
  • You provide services or do business in the EU
  • You export goods to the EU
  • You import goods from the EU

Check if you need to change your conformity assessment or conformity marking to sell your CE marked goods in the UK or EU

In most cases you can continue using the CE marking in the EU and UK (although in some cases you may need to transfer your certificate of conformity to an EU conformity asssesment body) - but if your good requires UKCA marking and you have not used it, then it will not be valid for sale in the UK.

Read the guidance: Prepare to use the UKCA mark after Brexit

Check if your employees need a visa or work permit and meet any requirements for their profession to work in the country they’re going to

You or your employees may not be able to enter or work in some countries.

Read the guidance: Providing services to any country in the EU, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway or Switzerland after Brexit

Selling goods and services in the EU

Because you said:

  • You own or operate a business or organisation
  • You provide services or do business in the EU
  • You export goods to the EU
  • You import goods from the EU

Check if you need to appoint a representative in the EU, and label your goods with your EU importer's details

If you do not meet the requirements, you may not be able to export goods to the EU.

Read the guidance: Placing manufactured goods on the EU market after Brexit

Check how to label food if you’re selling it in the UK or EU

You may not be able to sell goods in the EU if they’re labelled incorrectly.

Read the guidance: Food labelling changes if there’s a no-deal Brexit

Your industry

Because you said:

  • You own or operate a business or organisation
  • You work in agriculture and farming

Appoint a representative in the EU if you run a large UK-based online business providing digital services in the EU

You may be fined if you do not have a representative to help you meet online security standards.

Read the guidance: NIS Regulations - what digital service providers in the UK should do if there’s no Brexit deal

Check the rules on moving plants and plant products between the UK and the EU

The plants or plant products may be delayed or rejected if you do not follow the right rules

Read the guidance: Importing and exporting plants and plant products if there’s a no deal Brexit

Employing EU citizens

Because you said:

  • You own or operate a business or organisation
  • You employ EU citizens

Ask your employees to check if they need to apply to the EU Settlement Scheme

Your employees may not be able to continue living or working in the UK as they do now, if they do not apply to the scheme.

Read the guidance: Apply to the EU Settlement Scheme (settled and pre-settled status)

EU funding

Because you said:

  • You own or operate a business or organisation
  • You receive EU government funding

Check what you need to do if you currently receive EU funding

Read the guidance: The Government’s guarantee for EU-funded programmes if there’s no Brexit deal
